A pressure vessel is a container designed to hold gases or liquids at a pressure substantially different from the ambient pressure. These vessels are essential components in industries such as oil and gas, chemical processing, power generation, and manufacturing.

  • Storage Vessels: Used to store gases or liquids at high pressures. Examples include gas cylinders, compressed air tanks, and propane tanks.
  • Heat Exchangers: Transfer heat between two or more fluids under pressure. Common in industries like chemical processing, power plants, and refrigeration.
  • Process Vessels: Hold and sometimes react chemicals under pressure. Examples include distillation columns, reactors, and separators.
  • Boilers: Generate steam by heating water under pressure. Widely used in power generation and heating systems.
Materials Used in Industrial Chimneys
  • Brick: Traditional material, still used for small and medium-sized chimneys. Suitable for lower-temperature operations.
  • Concrete: Commonly used for taller and larger industrial chimneys due to its durability and strength
  • Steel: Offers high strength-to-weight ratio and is often used in chimneys requiring lightweight construction.

Industrial chimneys, also known as flue gas stacks, are tall structures designed to vent smoke, gases, and combustion products from boilers, furnaces, or other industrial systems into the atmosphere.

Working Principle of an EOT Crane

An EOT Crane (Electric Overhead Traveling Crane) is a type of overhead crane commonly used in industrial environments for lifting and moving heavy materials.

  • Longitudinal Movement: The entire crane travels along the runway, typically covering the length of the workshop or factory.
  • Cross Travel: The trolley, which carries the hoist, moves laterally across the bridge girder.
  • Vertical Movement: The hoist raises or lowers the load, lifting it vertically.
